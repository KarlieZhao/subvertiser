There Is No Manifesto

Subvertiser is a revolt against the MBTA’s massive advertising program that runs across its facilities.

The MBTA makes about $30 million each year from advertising. Advertising, as stated on the T’s website, plays a significant role in keeping rides affordable and avoiding fare increases.

At the moment, the T’s advertising program includes Ad postings inside or outside of buses, trains, and trolleys; posters and digital screens in MBTA stations; Billboards, display panels and other assets.

That said, walking on or beneath the streets of Boston, the most common things that pop up in your views - are the MBTA’s advertisements!

The T occupies parts of the public space physically and mentally (by feeding the passengers and pedestrians advertisements), simply because it provides citizens public services. But the fact is the T owns the properties, the train stations, billboards and displays, but not the public space. It is not a must for the public to compromise and receive information that celebrates consumption, capitalism, sexism, and questionable values with deceptive visual pollution.

Similar ideas could be found in the work Addendum, a browser extension that replaces the ads on your web pages with artworks. The developers talked about their attitude towards online ads:
“you downloaded the page, and you own it. It’s yours and you can do whatever you want to it. Just like if you get a free newspaper, you can read it, or cut it up, or burn it. It’s your life and you have no legal obligation to look at every ad presented to you.”

However, in the city of Boston, we are not the rule-maker. We appreciate the MBTA maximizes its revenues from alternative sources to avoid fare increases, but we do NOT accept that the public areas of Boston are crammed with advertisements. Our campaign can only be done in a tactical way.

By adopting the web AR technology, we provide the train/bus riders an alternative experience to being compelled to view the highly capitalistic, consumeristic information from advertisers. Anyone who has a smartphone can scan the QR code in the corners of the ads, and enjoy a digital artwork, a story, some history that is presented at the original advertisement’s place. There are more possibilities than ads!
