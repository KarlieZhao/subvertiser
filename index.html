There Is No Manifesto

Here’s a quote from the developers of Addendum from the art organization Kadist, talking about the issues with webpage ads and their attitude towards it. “You downloaded the page, and you own it. It’s yours and you can do whatever you want to it. Just like if you get a free newspaper, you can read it, or cut it up, or burn it. It’s your life and you have no legal obligation to look at every ad presented to you.”

The T occupies parts of the public space physically (with the billboards and stuff) and mentally (by feeding its customers and pedestrians advertising information), simply because it provides citizens public services. But the fact is the T owns the properties (billboards, displays, etc.), but not the public space.

They are the T’s properties, those are their billboards. They can put misinformation or advertisement on it, but it is a shared public space and no one can make you look at that informations.
